Grids: let's get into the niddy GRID-y

Why Do We Use Grids?

  • Promote consistency within our designs
  • Properly align elements on our page
  • Provide ample spacing between content

What makes up a grid

A grid is made up of three pieces: columns, gutters, and margins

Columns

Standard for how website design is layed out. Content contained within to give appearance of organization

Gutters

Name given to the space between columns. This is the term for whitespace which allows for the website to flow better.

White space

There are two types of white space. Passive and active. Passive, or micro whitespace is given to the type of whitespace between letters. This can be changed with line-height and margin. Different types of fonts will also have different amounts built in to them.

Active whitespace, or macrowhite space is intentional. It is used to spread out different sections of the website and is what is referred to as gutters. Should be able to allow readers to quickly understand what they should be focusing on and what is just supplementary details.

Margins

Margins are the sides of the page and the indentation into the page. This can be set up as a seperate column. This can be used as an active whitespace to direct the user's attention to the page.
